Actions

Action games focus on real-time challenges that test a player's reflexes, hand-eye coordination, and reaction speed. These games often involve combat, platforming, or fast-paced movement through levels filled with enemies and obstacles. The emphasis is on physical skill rather than strategic planning or narrative depth. Many action games feature simple controls but demand precision and timing, making them accessible yet difficult to master.

Action-Adventures

Action-adventure games blend the real-time, reflex-based gameplay of action titles with exploration, storytelling, and puzzle-solving typical of adventure games. Players often control a character who fights enemies, navigates environments, and interacts with the world to uncover narrative elements or complete objectives. These games offer a balance between combat and thoughtful exploration, often with a strong focus on character progression or open-world design. They tend to emphasize immersion and atmosphere while maintaining engaging moment-to-moment gameplay.

Adventures

Adventure games emphasize storytelling, exploration, and puzzle-solving over combat or action. They often involve point-and-click mechanics, dialogue trees, and the collection and use of items to advance the plot. These games usually place the player in narrative-rich environments, requiring critical thinking and decision-making to progress. Unlike action-adventure games, traditional adventure titles de-emphasize real-time gameplay and instead focus on unfolding a story through character interactions and environmental clues.

Role-Play Games

RPGs are centered on character development, story progression, and decision-making, often within expansive and immersive worlds. Players typically assume the role of a character or party of characters, complete quests, and develop abilities over time via experience points and skill systems. RPGs emphasize choice, whether in how a character evolves, how the story unfolds, or how to approach problems. They often feature dialogue systems, inventories, and stat-based mechanics like strength or intelligence. RPGs can be turn-based, real-time, or somewhere in between, and may appear in both single-player and multiplayer formats.

Simulations

Simulation games aim to recreate real-world systems, experiences, or scenarios with a high degree of accuracy or believability. These games can simulate flying aircraft, managing cities, raising pets, or running businesses, and they range from realistic to whimsical in tone. Players often manage variables and systems over time, rather than engage in fast-paced action. The gameplay emphasizes control, optimization, and long-term planning over winning or losing in a traditional sense.

Strategies

Strategy games challenge players to plan, manage resources, and make decisions to achieve victory, often against opponents or in complex scenarios. They require critical thinking, foresight, and adaptability, with a focus on long-term success rather than immediate action. These games can be turn-based or real-time. Victory often comes through superior planning and execution rather than reflexes or speed.
