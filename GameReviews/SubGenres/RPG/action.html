Action Subgenres

Battle Royale: In this subgenre, players compete against each other in a large, open arena with the objective of being the last person or team standing. Typically featuring a shrinking safe zone and resource gathering, games like Fortnite and PUBG emphasize survival, strategic positioning, and combat skills.

Battle Royale

Fighting: Fighting games center on close-quarters combat between characters with unique abilities and movesets. Players engage in one-on-one or team-based battles, employing combos, counters, and special attacks.

Fighting

Platformer: Platformers focus on navigating complex environments by running, jumping, and climbing across various platforms. Often featuring a mix of puzzles and enemies, classic examples include Super Mario Bros. and Celeste, blending precise timing and dexterity.

Platformer

Shooter: Shooter games emphasize gun-based combat, either in first-person (FPS) or third-person (TPS) perspectives. Players engage in missions or multiplayer battles, using a variety of weapons to eliminate opponents.
