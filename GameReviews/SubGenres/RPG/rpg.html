Role-Play Games Subgenres

JRPG (Japanese Role-Playing Game) is a subgenre of role-playing games that originated in Japan and is known for its story-driven gameplay, turn-based combat, and character progression. These games often feature stylized anime-inspired visuals, party-based mechanics, and deep narrative elements. JRPGs typically follow a linear storyline with predefined characters and strong emotional themes.

JRPG

CRPGs are rooted in tabletop role-playing traditions and are known for deep narrative experiences, complex character creation, and tactical or turn-based combat systems. These games often include expansive dialogue trees, party management, and moral or narrative choices that significantly affect outcomes. Many CRPGs are isometric and provide a top-down perspective, allowing players to manage multiple characters simultaneously. Emphasis is placed on storytelling, exploration, and strategy, rather than fast reflexes.

COMPUTER ROLE-PLAY GAMES

Action RPGs prioritize real-time, fast-paced combat while still incorporating core RPG elements like leveling, skill trees, and equipment customization. The gameplay loop typically revolves around engaging in battles, collecting loot, and upgrading characters through experience points and gear. ARPGs often feature fluid controls, reaction-based mechanics, and a more immediate sense of player agency during combat. While story and world-building exist, they usually take a backseat to gameplay intensity and progression systems.

ACTION RPG

Dungeon Crawlers emphasize exploration of dungeons, often involving grid-based movement and procedurally generated levels. Gameplay is typically centered around combat, loot collection, and resource management, with a strong focus on tactical decision-making. Some dungeon crawlers are turn-based and methodical, while others are in real time and more action-oriented. They often include permadeath or roguelike elements to increase difficulty and replay value. The narrative may be minimal, instead placing emphasis on atmosphere and player progression through increasingly difficult environments.

DUNGEON CRAWLER

MMORPGs are large-scale online games where thousands of players share a persistent world, engage in quests, combat, crafting, and social interaction. Players create characters and join guilds or factions, often participating in cooperative or competitive multiplayer events. These games typically feature extensive progression systems, including levels, equipment, and skills, as well as rich lore and evolving game worlds. Social features—such as trading, chatting, and group raiding—are central to the experience. The genre can include PvE (player vs. environment) content like dungeons and story quests, as well as PvP (player vs. player) arenas and battlegrounds.
